## 5. Universal airless explanation: why a sprayer does not prime

The agent can explain this simply:

âPriming means the pump is successfully pulling paint or water from the bucket into the pump and sending it back through the return tube or forward to the hose. When an airless sprayer does not prime, the most common causes are dried paint, a blocked suction strainer, a blocked gun filter, a stuck inlet ball, a dirty outlet valve, a leaking prime valve, air entering through the suction tube, or material that is too thick or not strained.â

The agent should remember this core principle:

Most airless pumps rely on small check balls and seats. If the ball is stuck, dirty, or cannot seal properly against the seat, the pump cannot create suction or hold pressure.
