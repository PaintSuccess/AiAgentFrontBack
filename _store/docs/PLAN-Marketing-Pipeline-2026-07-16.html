Two findings that change the strategy:
1. This audience is phone-first, not email. Only 4 of 61 conversational contacts can be emailed at all. An email-led plan doesn't fit these people. (Omnisend's Shopify-sourced list is separate and larger — but it's a different audience from the one talking to us.)
2. WhatsApp is currently failing 41% of its settled sends. This needs fixing before any money goes into the channel. Likely causes: messaging outside the 24-hour window without an approved template, or Twilio geographic permissions. This is ours to investigate and needs nobody else.
The real bottleneck isn't sending — it's permission. 56 of 61 contacts have simply never been asked for consent. Broadcasting isn't blocked by missing software; it's blocked by having almost nobody who has opted in. Consent grows through conversations — which is exactly what this funnel does.

4. Three paths

These aren't competing options to pick between — they're a sequence. The real question is how far down it to commit today.

~days · unblocked

Path A — “Prove the loop”

What's already captured, plus reporting sales back to Meta, plus funnel guidance written into the AI's instructions — no rigid scenario engine, no video, no broadcast.

  • Gets you: a working ad → WhatsApp → AI → order path, with Meta's algorithm optimising against real sales.
  • Needs from you: nothing. No scenario document, not one video clip.
  • Why it's attractive: it tests whether this funnel actually converts for Paint Access before anyone invests in producing content. If it doesn't work, you find out in weeks for almost no cost.
  • Trade-off: the AI's funnel behaviour stays loose, and you can't measure stage-by-stage.
weeks · blocked on content

Path B — “Real funnel” (the thing you described on 16 July)

Path A, plus a proper scenario engine (tracked stages, qualification, lead score) and the media pipeline for video and PDF.

  • Gets you: exactly the scenario from the brief.
  • Needs from you: the scenario content and the clips. Both are content, not engineering — and no amount of building substitutes for them.
  • Sequencing: start A now regardless; B's engineering can't meaningfully begin until the scenario arrives.
later · start the paperwork now

Path C — “Multi-channel”

Path B, extended to Instagram DM and Facebook Messenger on the same system and the same scenario.

  • Why it's cheap after B: the inbox, takeover, consent and scenario are all channel-agnostic. Instagram is largely a second connector, not a second project — and a “Click to Instagram Direct” ad is the same funnel as WhatsApp.
  • Act now on one thing: Meta's review process gates both channels. It costs nothing to start and becomes the bottleneck if left until it's needed.

7. Money — as currently understood

  • Customer messages us first: free. Replies within 24 hours cost nothing.
  • Straight after an ad click: reportedly free for ~72 hours, even for templates — unverified.
  • Us starting a conversation later: paid, and marketing messages cost more than service ones, with no volume discount.
  • Omnisend: billed on contacts stored, not sent — so email is near-free at the margin, but a growing list costs more whether you email it or not.
The economics point the same way as the brief. This funnel is cheapest exactly when the customer starts the conversation — which is what an ad click is — and most expensive when we chase people with templates. So the thing you want (ads → WhatsApp) is also the cheap thing, and broadcast — the expensive thing — is the thing that isn't built. That's a fortunate alignment.

8. What we'd suggest next

  1. Fix the WhatsApp failure rate. Everything downstream runs on this channel, and it's the only item needing nobody's input but ours.
  2. Start Path A — reporting sales back to Meta. Small, unblocked, makes the ads self-improving from the first click.
  3. Send us the scenario and the clips — in parallel. They're the long poles, and Path B genuinely cannot start without them.
  4. Kick off the Meta review for Instagram/Messenger — free to start, pure waiting.
  5. Answer the quick ones: Omnisend plan, Klaviyo flows, Shopify Inbox, Meta Pixel.

What we'd avoid for now: building broadcast, segments or a campaign screen. Omnisend already covers email, and WhatsApp broadcast would be an expensive way to message people who mostly haven't opted in yet. Grow consent through conversations first — then broadcasting is worth building.
