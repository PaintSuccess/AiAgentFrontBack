Email marketing with Omnisend — what each plan would cost us

Paint Access — 22 July 2026. All prices read directly from Omnisend's own upgrade page for our account (not brochure prices), quoted at our real contact count.

Our audience today

  • 38,600 contacts in Omnisend, of which 30,668 are billable — paid plans are priced on this number
  • Only ~14,000 are actual email subscribers — the rest have no marketing consent and can't legally be emailed anyway, but still count toward the bill
  • Omnisend flags 5.26% of the list as invalid/spam-traps; our cleaned lists ("Super Safe", "Bounce/Spam Clean") already exist from the July clean-up
  • Realistic sending volume: 1–4 campaigns/month to the 14K list ≈ 14K–56K emails/month, plus small automation volume (abandoned cart, welcome)

The cost lever: Omnisend does not charge for unsubscribed contacts — but it does charge for "non-subscribed" ones (never asked). Over half our billable count is exactly that. Pruning them (~14–16K billable left) should drop Standard to roughly $150–190/mo ≈ $1,800–2,280/yr (estimate, not a quote — the exact tier shows in-app after pruning).

Bottom line for the decision: staying on Free means Omnisend effectively can't send campaigns at all (500/mo cap). Making it usable costs $2,890–4,553/year as-is, or roughly $1,800–2,300/year after pruning the un-emailable contacts — and most of that money pays for storing contacts, not sending. The same campaigns through Shopify's built-in email cost tens of dollars a year, with the trade-off that a person presses Send (which doubles as a safety check). What Omnisend's paid tiers genuinely add: hands-off automated sending and nicer built-in reporting.
