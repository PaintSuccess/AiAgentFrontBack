A-bis. New channel requests — feasibility (added 16 July)

The good news: adding a channel is now a relatively small piece of work. The shared platform already handles threading, matching a conversation to the right customer, AI replies, operator takeover, hand-back, consent, and the inbox screen — all channel-agnostic. A new channel mainly means teaching the system to receive and send on that platform. What varies enormously is what each platform's own rules allow — that's the real driver of cost and timeline below.

Instagram DM — the strongest candidate

Meta's own messaging API for Instagram. Requires an Instagram Business account linked to a Facebook Page, plus Meta's review of the integration before it can go live for real customers. Once live: the customer has to message first (no cold outreach), and there's a 24-hour free-reply window per conversation, same shape as WhatsApp's rule.

Why this matters most: a "Click to Instagram Direct" ad works exactly like the WhatsApp ad you described — click, land in a DM, our bot picks it up. If we build the funnel logic once for WhatsApp, Instagram is mostly a second connector on the same engine, not a second project. Strong candidate for a second phase right after WhatsApp.

Facebook Messenger — the easiest add

Two ways to do this: through the same Twilio account we already use for WhatsApp/SMS, or directly through Meta. Same 24-hour reply-window rule applies. If we end up integrating directly with Meta for Instagram anyway, it makes sense to do Messenger the same way at the same time — one review process, one integration, covering two channels.

Shopify Inbox — recommend against integrating; needs one answer from you

There is no public way for outside systems (including ours) to read or send Shopify Inbox conversations — Shopify doesn't offer that access. More importantly, Shopify Inbox does the same job our own inbox already does (it pulls together store chat, Messenger and email into one screen), so integrating it would mean running two overlapping inboxes rather than gaining a channel.

The one thing we do need from you: is Shopify Inbox chat currently switched on for the storefront? If it is, any conversations happening there are invisible to us and to the AI right now — worth knowing either way. If it's on and not actively needed, turning it off is simpler than building a bridge to it.

TikTok Messages — possible, but not yet confirmed

TikTok does have a business messaging API, but access is granted case-by-case to approved partners rather than being open for anyone to use — so we can't commit to a timeline until we know whether approval is realistic. Availability is by region; Australia is one of the eligible ones. We'd treat this as a short research step to find out what's actually possible, rather than a committed build, until that's answered. Worth also confirming whether TikTok ads are even part of the plan — if not, this can wait.

Suggested order, if all four move forward:
  1. WhatsApp / Click-to-WhatsApp — already in progress
  2. Instagram DM — shares the funnel logic built for WhatsApp; needs Meta's review process, which has its own lead time, so worth starting early
  3. Facebook Messenger — a cheap add-on once the Meta review for Instagram is underway
  4. Shopify Inbox — confirm whether it's on, decide whether to keep it, not something to integrate
  5. TikTok — a short feasibility check, not a committed build, until partner access is confirmed

Summary — what's actually new work

Everything above this line is either already built, already covered by an external tool, or deliberately on hold. Stripping that away, the WhatsApp-ad funnel you described on 16 July needs four genuinely new pieces of work:

1. Ad attribution. Recognise which Meta ad a WhatsApp conversation came from, store it against the conversation, and report leads/purchases back to Meta so the ad campaign can optimise on real outcomes rather than just "conversation started." Twilio already gives us the information we need for this — we're just not capturing it yet. Small, contained, high leverage.
2. The funnel/scenario itself. Structured stages — greeting, qualifying questions, recommendation, quote, checkout — with the AI tracking where each customer is in that journey. This is blocked on getting the actual scenario content from you: the questions to ask, how to qualify a lead, which product path follows which answer, and which asset (link, video, PDF) belongs at which step.
3. Media delivery. Reversing the earlier decision to keep the inbox text-only, and building the video/PDF pipeline that was already designed in June. Blocked on your side producing the 20–50 short clips the original plan called for — the technical pipeline to deliver them is the smaller half of this item.
4. Enforcement + measurement. Making the consent rule actually block non-consented marketing sends (not just warn), and populating the event log so the funnel can be measured end to end. Needed before any broadcast-style sending goes live, and before we can report back on how well the funnel is performing.
Also worth fixing regardless of the above: the AI currently tells website visitors "I've shown that on your screen" for both order details and a WhatsApp handoff button — on the website widget, neither actually appears. Independent of the funnel work, but worth closing since it's a live, if minor, broken promise to customers today.
